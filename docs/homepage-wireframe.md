# TurboGixxer V2 Homepage Wireframe

## Homepage Job

The homepage should feel like a signature brand experience. Its job is to communicate recognition, control, and authority, then route visitors into services, builds, shop, contact, or booking.

## Experience Principles

- Cinematic, not cluttered
- Aggressive, but controlled
- Technical, but premium
- Memorable, but still legible
- Contact and booking must remain easy to reach

## Section Order

1. Hero Sequence
2. Recognition Strip
3. Service Path Section
4. Featured Builds Preview
5. Shop Preview
6. About / Legacy Preview
7. Final Booking and Contact CTA

## 1. Hero Sequence

### Purpose

Create a strong first impression and establish TurboGixxer as a recognized, premium performance brand.

### Content

- Main headline
- Short brand statement
- Primary CTAs
- High-impact media

### CTAs

- Book
- Contact
- View Builds

### Motion Direction

- Full-screen GSAP intro
- Layered media movement
- Controlled headline reveal
- Optional pinned scroll beat
- Motion should feel engineered, not decorative

## 2. Recognition Strip

### Purpose

Translate brand recognition into quick proof points immediately after the hero.

### Content Options

- years of experience
- major ECU platforms
- tuning philosophy statement
- recognition/reputation stats if credible

### Design Direction

- compressed, technical, precise
- can feel like telemetry or instrument readout

## 3. Service Path Section

### Purpose

Help users choose their path without forcing them to read too much.

### Cards

- Dyno
- Remote
- Project Review

### Each Card Should Include

- short title
- one-sentence fit statement
- short support line
- CTA

### CTAs

- View Dyno
- View Remote
- Start Review

### Motion Direction

- staggered reveal
- interactive hover transitions
- optional pinned comparison transition

## 4. Featured Builds Preview

### Purpose

Show proof and visual authority.

### Structure

- section intro
- 3 to 6 featured build cards
- one hero build can receive larger treatment

### Build Card Content

- image
- build title
- platform
- short summary

### CTA

- View All Builds

### Motion Direction

- image masking
- reveal sequencing
- selective parallax

## 5. Shop Preview

### Purpose

Show that the shop exists, but keep it curated and brand-aligned.

### Structure

- split into Merch and Haltech
- curated preview items
- short framing copy

### CTAs

- Shop Merch
- Shop Haltech

### Motion Direction

- simpler than hero and builds
- should support browsing, not overpower it

## 6. About / Legacy Preview

### Purpose

Explain who TurboGixxer has been and why the brand matters.

### Content

- concise brand history
- philosophy statement
- who the customer is

### CTA

- Read More

### Motion Direction

- restrained
- text and image choreography
- tone should feel established, not loud

## 7. Final Booking and Contact CTA

### Purpose

End the homepage with an obvious next step.

### Content

- short direct CTA statement
- booking CTA
- contact CTA

### CTAs

- Start Booking
- Contact TurboGixxer

### Motion Direction

- strong but simple
- avoid complicated effects that slow conversion

## Homepage Content Rules

- The homepage should not become a dump of every service detail.
- The homepage should preview, not explain everything.
- The homepage must always keep at least one clear booking path visible.
- Featured builds and shop previews should remain curated and selective.

## Homepage Non-Goals

- full service documentation
- full shop inventory display
- long-form biography
- article-heavy content

## Creative Direction Reference

Recommended theme:

`Legacy Under Load`

Meaning:
- the brand has history
- the work is serious
- the motion language should suggest force, control, and precision
